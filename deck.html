01 / Problem
Sending money home costs
a fortune.

The fee problem

Average global remittance fee is 6.5%

On a $300 transfer — the most common amount — that's $19.50 that never reaches the family. World Bank data, Q4 2024.

The opacity problem

Hidden FX spreads add another 2–4% on top

Most services advertise "low fees" but claw it back with unfavorable exchange rates. Senders rarely realize the true cost.

The experience problem

Slow, clunky UX designed for compliance, not people

Western Union and MoneyGram were built for agents and tellers. Their apps show it. Diaspora users deserve better.

$800B Global remittance flows annually
$48B Lost to fees & FX spreads every year
4.5M Ghanaian diaspora globally
02 / Solution
A remittance app built
with love.

Zero transfer fees, always

We make money on interchange revenue from the debit card we issue, not on fees. What senders see is what arrives.

Mid-market exchange rate, disclosed upfront

No hidden FX spread. We show the exact rate before confirmation and lock it for 60 seconds.

Give Back — charity built into every send

Users can optionally round up or add a fixed amount to support vetted charities in Ghana. No guilt, just impact.

3-tap transfers, 15-second onboarding

Built for people who send home monthly, not quarterly. Saved recipients, one-tap repeat sends, instant confirmations.

Why Ghana. Why now.

Diaspora concentration

Corridor growth

Ghana received $4.7B in remittances in 2024 — up 11% YoY

Ghana is Sub-Saharan Africa's fastest-growing remittance corridor. Mobile money penetration (MoMo) enables instant delivery.

Regulatory tailwind

Ghana's BoG actively encouraging fintech innovation

The Bank of Ghana's regulatory sandbox and FCA Fast Track (UK) give Akoma a clear licensing pathway in key markets.

05 / Business Model
We make money.
Just not from our users.

Primary (Today)

Debit card interchange revenue

Akoma issues a co-branded Visa debit card to senders. We earn 1.5% interchange on every card swipe. Projected: $18 ARPU at 100K users.

Secondary (Q3 2025)

Give Back platform fee

Charities pay a 3% platform fee on donations processed through Akoma — far below Stripe Donate or Benevity. Projected: $4 ARPU at 100K users.

Future (2026+)

Financial services expansion

Savings accounts, credit products, and cross-border insurance for the diaspora. Leverages existing customer identity and trust.
