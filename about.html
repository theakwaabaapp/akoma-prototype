How it started

The story behind Akoma

Kwame Boateng grew up watching his parents lose money to wire transfer fees. Each month, a portion of what they worked hard to earn simply vanished — absorbed by middlemen, exchange rate spreads, and charges nobody explained.

In 2023, after sending $500 home for a family emergency and learning his mother had received the equivalent of $460, he decided enough was enough. He called his former Stripe colleague Ama Sarpong that evening, and by the end of the call, Akoma was taking shape on a shared document.

The name means "heart" in Akan — because every transfer should feel like an act of love, not a transaction. Today, Akoma processes thousands of transfers monthly with zero fees and an optional contribution to a cause the sender cares about.

What we stand for

Our values

Radical transparency

No hidden fees, ever. What you see is what your family gets. We publish our rates, margins, and methodology openly.

Community first

Built with, not just for, the diaspora. Every feature comes from real feedback. Our product roadmap is shaped by the people who use Akoma daily.

Impact by default

Every transfer is an opportunity to give back. We make it effortless to support schools, clinics, and communities in Ghana with every send.
